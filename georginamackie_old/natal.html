Nutrition

Gaining the right amount of weight during pregnancy by eating a healthy, balanced diet is a good sign that your baby is getting all the nutrients they need to develop at a healthy rate. It is not necessary to "eat for two" during pregnancy. It's true that you need extra calories from nutrient-rich foods to help your baby grow, but you generally need to consume only 300 more calories than you did before you became pregnant to meet the needs of your growing baby.

Knowing what to eat and when can help ease morning sickness and other unpleasant side-effects of pregnancy.
